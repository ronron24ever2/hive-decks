The European Squeeze

Europe isn't short on operators. It's short on margin.

The usual autonomy pitch is "we can't find skilled workers." That matters, but the pressures European customers are actually feeling run deeper. Four forces are pushing them toward physical AI right now.

Margin squeeze Contractors need lower operating cost per hour European construction and aggregates margins are thinner than they have been in a decade. Physical AI compresses the largest variable cost line, operator cost per productive hour, without touching the machine spec customers already trust.
Chinese push Mid-segment price pressure is already here Chinese OEMs are winning mid-segment European deals on price. Dealers are increasingly indifferent to origin. A differentiator that is not price, and that customers cannot get from a Chinese supplier, is worth building around.
Dealer aftermarket Physical AI is a new recurring revenue stream Your dealers already earn more on aftermarket than on machine sales. Physical AI as a service plugs directly into that model: a new subscription line the dealer network can sell and support alongside parts and maintenance.
Market perception Volvo's physical AI position is unclear in the market In customer conversations across Nordics and Europe, Hive finds that buyers cannot name a single current Volvo physical AI offering. Peers are very visible. Volvo is not. That gap is both a risk and an opportunity to move fast.
Hive's vision extends beyond Europe. We are actively expanding to the US market, working with major companies in Texas including one of the world's largest energy producers. We follow customers where Volvo's customers are.

Human-in-the-loop by design
