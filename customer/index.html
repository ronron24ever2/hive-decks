What We Hear From Every Customer

Four pressures. One common pattern.

Margin squeeze The math that used to work no longer does Thinner margins driven by energy costs, fewer contracts, and a construction market that is not booming. Less volume to sell, same fixed costs. You need lower cost per productive hour just to stay competitive.
Operator pressure Repetitive, heavy, hard to fill 12-hour shifts on the same task, day after day. The boring jobs have the highest turnover. You are overworking the people you have, and the ones you lose are the hardest to replace.

Appendix: Discovery Checklist

Questions to build the case.

01
The Operation
  1. What machines do you run? Which models, which years?
  2. What is the primary task? (load-haul-dump, crusher feeding, stockpile management)
  3. How many shifts are you running today? How many could you run?
  4. What connectivity do you have on site? (4G/5G, WiFi)
  5. Is there someone on site daily for machine inspection?
02
The Economics
  1. What does a fully loaded operator cost you per hour? Per year?
  2. How many operators do you have on wheel loaders today?
  3. What happens when you cannot fill a shift? (lost revenue, reduced output)
  4. What is your current cost per tonne / per productive hour?
03
The Decision
  1. Who approves spend on operational technology? (Economic Buyer)
  2. What is the internal process from "yes" to signed contract?
  3. Who is the internal champion for this initiative?
  4. Have you evaluated autonomy or remote operation before? What happened?
04
Pilot Setup
  1. When would you want to start a pilot? (Compelling Event / Timeline)
  2. What does success look like after 90 days?
  3. Are there site-specific safety requirements or third-party approvals needed?
  4. What KPIs matter most to your management? (cost/tonne, utilization, safety)
